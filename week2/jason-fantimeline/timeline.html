Tommy Hilfiger

An American fashion designer best known for founding the lifestyle brand
Tommy Hilfiger Corporation in 1985. Hilfiger's collections are often influenced by
the fashion of music subcultures and marketed in connection with the music industry.

1985: Starting the Corporation

In 1985, he founded the Tommy Hilfiger Corporation with support from The Murjani Group. The new clothing line made its debut with a high-profile marketing campaign, in Times Square designed by George Lois.

1992: Gaining Recognition

The Tommy Hilfiger Corporation went public in 1992, introducing Hilfiger's signature menswear collection. Hilfiger was named Menswear Designer of the Year by the Council of Fashion Designers of America in 1995.

1996: Going Into A New Market

After licensing Pepe Jeans USA in 1995, in 1996, Tommy Hilfiger Inc. began distributing women's clothing.

1997 - 1998: Going Global

Hilfiger had opened his first store in Beverly Hills, which was followed by a store in London in 1998. Hilfiger was serving as the company's co-chairman by 1997, and that year he published his first book, titled All American: "A Style Book".

Recent Years

In recent years Tommy Hilfiger has worked with many different artists such as, Beyonce, Marc Anthony, and Jennifer Lopez. In 2012, Hilfiger was awarded the Geoffrey Beene Lifetime Achievement Award from the Council of Fashion Designers of America. Global sales in retail for the brand in 2013 were US $6.4 billion, and $6.7 billion in 2014. Tommy Hilfigers clothing also managed to stay relevant in streetwear culture today because of its vintage pieces.
